# Editorial Style Wiki

## What works (keep doing)
- Lead with the specific verified development plainly; concrete news beats broad setup.
- Keep the headline and intro aligned on the same core fact, but make each line add something new rather than repeating it.
- Use a clear team/player connection as the anchor for insider or honorific news when the source supports it.
- Stay tightly grounded in the source even when the story invites bigger significance.
- Keep Hall of Fame, retirement, or announcement pieces focused on the actual action being reported, not the surrounding era or identity framing.
- Direct, news-first phrasing tends to work better than generic label-heavy copy.
- When the source supports a single core development, build around that and stop there.
- A concrete team/player hook and clear decision-point framing can carry a thin source digest without overreaching.
- [insider] A verified team-linked milestone or career announcement can carry the piece if the lead names it directly and cleanly.
- [columnist] A formal honor works best when the opening makes the team’s action the hook, not just the subject’s fame.

## What to avoid
- Don’t lead with broad identity or era language when the source gives a more concrete development.
- Don’t make the headline and intro say the same thing in slightly different words.
- Don’t rely on generic phrasing when the source supports a sharper tap reason.
- Don’t pad a simple honor, retirement, or announcement with extra context that isn’t doing new work.
- Don’t widen a focused team/player item into bigger significance unless the source actually supplies it.
- Don’t let the opening stack headline, subhead, and intro with the same announcement three times.
- Don’t blur a concrete development into a vague “set for” or similar soft framing when the action is firmer.
- Don’t treat source-backed grounding as enough by itself if the execution is repetitive.
- Don’t force multiple angles from one clear fact pattern.
- Don’t drift beyond a thin source digest just to manufacture extra substance.
- [insider] Don’t bury the actual honor, milestone, or retirement under generic connection-first framing.
- [columnist] Don’t lean on source-narration or meta-style filler when a cleaner, more direct hook is available.
